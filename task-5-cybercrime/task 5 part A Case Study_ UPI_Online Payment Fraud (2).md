***Case Study: UPI/Online Payment Fraud***

UPI fraud is a common cybercrime where attackers trick users into transferring money or revealing sensitive banking information. This type of fraud has increased with the growing use of digital payment apps.

In a realistic scenario, a college student named Rahul wanted to sell his old phone online. A fraudster contacted him pretending to be a genuine buyer and agreed to pay the amount through UPI. The attacker sent a fake payment request link and guided Rahul to “accept” the payment. However, instead of receiving money, Rahul unknowingly approved a request to send money from his own account. In another step, the fraudster asked for an OTP, claiming it was needed to complete the transaction. Trusting the caller, Rahul shared the OTP, which resulted in additional unauthorized deductions.

Young students and people unfamiliar with digital payment systems are often targeted in such scams. The consequences include financial loss, stress, and loss of trust in online systems.

This situation could have been avoided if Rahul had verified the payment request carefully and remembered that receiving money does not require entering a PIN or OTP. Users should never share OTPs or approve unknown payment requests. Awareness and careful usage of digital platforms are essential to prevent such frauds.
